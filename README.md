# STREET PLAYER

A turn based street trading game for the Apple II, written in Applesoft BASIC.
Needs an 80 column display (IIe with the 80 column card, or a IIc/IIgs). The
program runs PR#3 on startup.

## Running it

Paste or EXEC the listing into Applesoft and RUN. On a DOS 3.3 disk named
HELLO, it autoruns on boot.

## The hustle

You are a street hustler. You buy stock, you hold it, and you sell it, and the
whole game is in when. On the side you add shops to your book. A shop in
trouble takes your money to clear what it owes, and after that it pays you
every week for as long as you have a guy to go and collect.

Every action costs you a day. Prices and demand shift daily. Protection,
payroll and loan collections settle once a week.

## Commands

- (B)uy: stock up. Bulk discounts at 100, 500 and 5000 units.
- (S)ell: limited by demand. No buyers, no sale.
- (L)oan: borrow money, or pay back what you owe.
- (P)rotection: add shops that pay you every week.
- (M)uscle: hire guys. They guard your stash, collect from your shops and your
  loans, and pull jobs.
- (W)ait: let a day pass. Shit happens on days you do nothing.
- (R)etire: cash out, if you are clean enough to be allowed to.

## The cops

Sooner or later the cops come to you wanting money to look the other way. Pay
and they leave you alone for a while, though it wears off. Refuse, or come up
short, and you are on their list, and staying on it is what gets you raided.
Buying big draws their attention. So does pulling a job.

## The competition

Your guys know where rival stashes are. It takes 3 guys 3 days to take down a
competitor's stash house. They come back with product you did not pay for, or
they do not come back at all.

It runs both ways. The competition knows where your stash is too, and the only
thing between them and it is muscle.

## Things that will kill you

- The cops raid you while you are on their list.
- You miss a weekly payment on money you borrowed.
- Someone turns over your stash and finds nothing worth taking.

## Notes

You can collect from one shop and one loan yourself. Anything past that needs a
guy, one per shop and one per loan. Your guys cover the shops first, and
whoever is left over works the loan book. Anything you cannot cover goes
uncollected, and a loan nobody collects just keeps growing.

Sending muscle to do a robbery takes them away from guarding your own stash and
collecting for you. Even if they succeed, you might still lose a guy, and the
cops will always look for a bribe to look the other way afterwards.

Retirement values your stash only up to what the street will take that day.
Anything above that is worthless, and money still out on loan is written off.

## Someday

- Shops come to you, rather than you going out and adding them.
